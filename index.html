Bio

Projects

1-bit Phase-Shifting Switch Module for Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS)

I led an undergraduate capstone on a 1-bit phase-shifting switch module for RIS, optimizing transistor sizing and matching networks to achieve low insertion loss and a stable 180° phase shift over a wide bandwidth. The work was recognized with an IEEE AP-S Undergraduate Research Scholarship and presented at KIEES (2025).

Paper
1.5- / 2-bit Phase-Shifting Switch Module for RIS (In Progress)

Building on the 1-bit design, I am extending the architecture to 1.5-/2-bit switching to reduce quantization loss and improve beam control. My current focus is comparing phase resolution, insertion loss, and implementation complexity to select a practical multi-bit topology for RIS front-ends.

Audio Equalizer Design

I built a multiband audio equalizer by composing LPF/BPF/HPF sections, a summing amplifier, and a push-pull output stage. The circuit was verified in PSPICE and then prototyped on a breadboard with a volume controller for gain tuning and listening tests.

Infrared Sensor PCB Design

I designed and fabricated a compact IR-sensor board in Eagle CAD, creating custom libraries, completing schematic and layout, generating Gerber files, and assembling the prototype to validate the signal-conditioning chain.

FPGA-Based Barnsley Fern Visualization

I implemented Barnsley-fern fractals on a Pynq-Z2 using Verilog, driving an LCD via a VGA pipeline. An FSM varies the iteration count via buttons, and RGB pixel data are rendered from BRAM to achieve real-time visualization.

Patch Antenna Design

I designed a 10-GHz microstrip patch antenna, progressing from CST EM modeling to Eagle CAD layout and adopting an open-stub matching network to meet the return-loss target. Guided by CST simulations, I balanced gain, efficiency, footprint, and matching (S11) to reach a practical design point.

Digital Sequential Circuit Design: Vending Machine & 3-Way Traffic Light Controller

I implemented two sequential digital systems: a vending-machine controller (Moore FSM) and a T-junction traffic-light controller (counter-based phases). Both were synthesized from K-maps to a PLA/D-flip-flop design and verified in simulation.

Samsung Shining Star Program

A five-day immersion hosted by Samsung Semiconductor for early-year undergraduates. I led a small team through technical tasks and briefs while learning core semiconductor technology through lectures by the Samsung Institute of Technology and on-site visits to semiconductor business units.
